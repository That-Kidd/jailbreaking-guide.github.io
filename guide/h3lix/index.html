H3lix Jailbreak Method

This method only supports 32-Bit Devices on iOS 10.0 - iOS 10.3.3. If you are using a 64-bit device please use DoubleH3lix instead.
Before continuing this guide, please make sure you have backed up your iOS Device via iTunes and iCloud.
For this guide you will need to following;
  • The H3lix IPA
  • Cydia Impactor
  • A Free Developer Apple ID
Step 1: Cydia Impactor
  1. Connect your iOS device to your Mac or PC (Apple OEM cables are recommended)
  2. Start Cydia Impactor, then drag the H3lix IPA onto the window
  3. You will be prompted for your Apple ID credentials (this one has to be enrolled in a beta program (free or paid)
  4. Once you've entered your credentials in correctly, Cydia Impactor should install H3lix onto your iOS Device.
If this fails, try again with your correct Apple ID or a different charge/sync cable.
Step 2: H3lix Jailbreak
  1. Once the H3lix App has been installed onto your iOS Device, make sure to delete all OTA updates if any
  2. Open Settings then navigate to General > Profiles & Device Management. The Apple ID used to install the app should show.
  3. Tap on the Profile, then tap Verify (or Trust) and follow all prompts onscreen
  4. Now that H3lix has been verified, open it on your Home Screen
  5. Once open, tap Run uichache and follow all prompts onscreen
If the Jailbreak fails, reboot your device and try again. You can also refer to Troubleshooting
Step 3: Cydia
  1. After your device resprings, you should have Cydia installed on your Home Screen
  2. Launch Cydia and let it (re)load its packages. If you are prompted for an upgrade, tap Upgrade All
If Cydia crashes, open H3lix and tap Run uichache. Once your device resprings, you should be able to open Cydia

Once all upgrades are completed and you've resprung, you are free to use any (compatible) Cydia tweaks

If Cydia or any tweaks installed from Cydia start crashing after a respring or reboot, open H3lix and then tap Run uichache. Your device should be re-jailbroken after the respring.
