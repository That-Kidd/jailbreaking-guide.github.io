Blobs

What are blobs?

A blob is a term for a small piece of data that is part of Apple's digital signature protocol for iOS restores and updates, designed to control the iOS versions that users can install on their iOS devices. This generally only allows the newest iOS version to be installed.



Why would I want to save blobs?

If a jailbreak comes out for a newer version that is more stable than yours, you can just restore to that version if yo have saved the blobs.



How does saving blobs work?

The latest ios versions are signed for a short period of time this can range from 1 to 4 weeks. You can check the signing status of every ios version here. If some ios version is signed latest or not you can save the blob for that version what will be explained later on.



How can i save blobs?

You will need the following;
  • A Computer
  • iTunes
Step 1: Device info
  1. Connect your iOS device to your Mac or PC (Apple OEM cables are recommended)
  2. Start Itunes and press on the phone icon and go to the summary of your iOS
  3. Click on the serial number of your iOS to find out your ECID and write it down
  4. Click again to find your model identifier and write it down
  5. Convert your ECID to Hex by inputing it here.
Step 2: Saving the blobs
  1. Go on TSS Saver.
  2. Put in your ECID and your iDevice identifier
  3. Click submit and download your blobs somewhere save.

We recommend double saving them in a cloud and locally.
