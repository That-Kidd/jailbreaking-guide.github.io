Jailbreaking FAQ



What is Jailbreaking?

Jailbreaking is an exploit used to elevate permissions and gain root access to iOS. This allows installation of apps and tweaks.



What iOS versions are supported?

Not all versions of iOS are supported and not all devices are supported on certain versions. To check which Jailbreak supports your device click here.



Is it possible to remove a Jailbreak?

Yes it is possible to remove a Jailbreak, either by restoring to an iTunes backup or by updating to an unsupported version of iOS.



How can I remove my Jailbreak?

For Jailbroken devices on iOS 7.1 - 10.3.3 you can use Cydia Eraser If you are using BytaFont, it is best to uninstall before using this. For devices running iOS 11, you can restore to an iTunes backup, restore the Factory Settings using iTunes, or update ot an unsupported version of iOS.

Itunes method

First of all, connect your device to your computer, open iTunes, click on the device and backup your data. This will allow you to restore your data back onto your device if you choose to do so. In iTunes, click on "Restore iPhone" (this will depend on your device â€“ it could be iPad, iPhone or iPod). This will ask you if you want to restore and update to the latest iOS. Once complete, your device will be restore to the latest iOS. At this point you'll get an option, either setup as new which won't put any of your data back onto your device, or restore backup which will put your data back onto your device.
