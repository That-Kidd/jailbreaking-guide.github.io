Jailbreaking FAQ



What is jailbreaking?

iOS jailbreaking is privilege escalation for the purpose of removing software restrictions imposed by Apple on iOS, tvOS and watchOS.It typically does this by using a series of kernel patches.



What iOS version should I be on so I can use the next jailbreak?

Nobody knows which iOS version/s will be supported by the next jailbreak. It just depends on what exploits are found and which versions they support.



Is it possible to remove a jailbreak temporary?

Yes, most jailbreaks are semi-tethered so you cann just reboot your iDevice and your jailbreak is gone if you want to jailbreak it again simply run the app again you jailbroke with.



How can i remove the jailbreak from my phone?

If your device is running iOS 7.1 - 10.3.3 you can use Cydia ereaser found here Cydia Eraser will wipe the device to factory settings and remove the jailbreak on the device but keeping it on the same iOS version so you can jailbreak again. Note: some people report that if you have changed a font with BytaFont, it can cause Cydia Eraser to fail and ruin the jailbreak unless you revert the font to stock first.



Itunes Method

First of all, connect your device to your computer, open iTunes, click on the device and backup your data. This will allow you to restore your data back onto your device if you choose to do so. In iTunes, click on "Restore iPhone" (this will depend on your device – it could be iPad, iPhone or iPod). This will ask you if you want to restore and update to the latest iOS. Once complete, your device will be restore to the latest iOS. At this point you'll get an option, either setup as new which won't put any of your data back onto your device, or restore backup which will put your data back onto your device.
